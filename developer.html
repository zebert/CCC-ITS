Overview

Reasons to attend CCC?
  • Our hands-on approach to learning allows students to immerse themselves in the latest technologies. Our Developer specialization uses real world projects. Student can work with real clients and complete projects that can be placed in a portfolio for when they apply for jobs, giving them a leg up over other students from other programs.
  • Student also work with clients/co-workers from various locations. The Developer specialization uses distance technologies for students to collaborate with other students from various campuses. This helps them prepare and practice their online meeting skills as well as distance collaboration skills. These are valuable skills that employers are looking for!
What are the major skills a student will learn from your program?
  • There are many skills that INFO Technology and Systems students will learn including: problem solving, web and application development, troubleshooting, database management, project management, planning, research, and customer service.
  • Software Developers are the creative minds behind computer programs. Some develop the applications that allow people to do specific tasks on a computer or another device. Others develop the underlying systems that run the devices or that control networks according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/software-developers.htm).
What additional program costs are there?
  • Students in the Developer specialization are required to purchase their own web hosting site. Cost for this can vary from $0 to $100 per year. Students typically chose the plan that is best for them and most pay a monthly fee around $7 per month.
  • Microsoft Office 365 software is provided free from the college. This software includes Word, Excel, Access, PowerPoint, and OneDrive.
  • Students also have access to our Virtual Desktop Interface which allows students to remotely log into our server and access software provided by the college.
  • Information Technology and Systems program provides students taking an INFO course the ability to download Microsoft software for free from our Imagine Academy. This software includes a majority of Microsoftâ€™s software including Windows, Visual Studio, Project, Visio, etc.
  • Most other software required in the program is open source software which is available for free to download.

Careers

What are the starting wages of graduates?

  • Software Development has been in the top 5 jobs for the past few years according to US News and World Report. According to their 2018 report, Software Developers are the number 1 job. See the following URL for more information. https://www.usnews.com/info/blogs/press-room/articles/2018-01-10/us-news-announces-the-2018-best-jobs
  • The median salary from this report is $100,080, however most students entering in the work force earn around $45,000 - $55,000 for entry-level work and progress to the median salary throughout their career.

What local employers or potential employers hire your students?

  • Students with the Developer specialization have received employment in a number of various companies in many locations across the state. A few examples are Xpanion in Kearney, City of Hastings, Platte Valley Communications in Kearney, Mid America Computing Company of Blair, Hastings Public Schools, Educational Service Unit, Gallop of Omaha, Becton-Dickinson - Holdrege.

What career possibilities are available for students earning a:

Certificate
This 12 credit option is a great advantage for students who may already have a degree or experience in another field. Adding a certificate can enhance your employability.
Diploma
This one year, 30 credit option enables students to pursue work in the industry. This option includes 9 credits of General Education courses and 21 credits in Information Technology and Systems courses.
Degree
The best option for students to pursue a career in Information Technology. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, Information Technology occupations are expected to grow at 13%.

Program Options

The Information Technology & Systems Degree provides a comprehensive education in the skills, techniques and methodologies of information technology and systems. Graduates of the Information Technology & Systems program will be able to provide effective IT functions to solve business problems using best practice methodologies. Students can specialize in one of the following areas: Developer, Networking, and Technical Support.

Questions/Answers

When can I start classes?
Check the college calendar for specific dates, however classes begin in the fall, spring and summer semesters.
How long will it take to earn my degree?
Students pursuing their degree full time can expect to complete their degree in two years.
Can I go full or part-time?
Students can pursue their degree part time. Be sure to check course availability as not all courses are offered each term.
Can I work and attend classes?
Students can work while attending classes however we recommend not working over 20 hours per week to allow enough time for class and homework.
How long will I be in classes each day?
Class sessions vary by specialization and delivery method. For students attending lectures, we try to schedule the Developer courses on M-W-F in the morning.
Do I have to attend class at a certain location or online?
Each course is different and depending on the delivery method you chose would dictate the class attendance requirements.
